> [!quote] Field Note
> People adapt better when support is ready before pressure becomes a crisis. Service-minded leadership builds the reserve, relationship, and practice space before the hard moment arrives.

> [!summary] The Reader Promise
> Today's field note is about margin of safety. The lesson is simple: do not wait for the forecast to fail before building the support people will need.

## The Field Note

> [!example] Ready Before It Breaks
> A service system needs trusted local partners before benefits are disrupted.
>
> A founder needs reachable advice before the first hard month turns into isolation.
>
> A student or manager needs practice, guidance, and backup capacity before pressure becomes failure.

Good support is not only a rescue plan. It is a margin of safety.

That means building room for error before the system is under stress. A community organization needs trusted partners before a shutdown threatens food access. A founder needs advice close enough to use before cash, customers, or confidence get tight. A manager needs time and recognition before change work becomes another invisible burden. Students need practice and support paths before a transition becomes a crisis.

The leadership move is practical. Ask where the first strain will show up, then build the reserve there. The reserve might be a partner list, a manager check-in, a practice assignment, a clear support channel, or a person whose quiet coordination keeps work moving.

Service becomes real when people do not need perfect forecasts to stay supported.

## Mental Model Lens

> [!tip] Thinking Tool: Margin of Safety
> **Mental model:** [Margin of Safety](https://fs.blog/margin-of-safety/)  
> **Useful idea:** A margin of safety is extra room for error. It protects people when plans are wrong, demand rises, or stress arrives sooner than expected.  
> **Short quote:** "The purpose of the margin of safety is to render the forecast unnecessary."  
> **Use today:** Ask what backup capacity already exists before people need it: reserve time, trusted relationships, practice, partner support, or a clear path to help.

> [!note] Why It Matters
> Margin of safety keeps service work honest. It moves leaders away from hopeful plans and toward support that still works when conditions change.

## Use It Today

> [!question] For Students
> What support would help you before a class, job, or project gets hard? Can you name the person, place, or practice that gives you room to recover?

> [!question] For Managers And Founders
> Where is your work depending on one person, one tool, or one rushed decision? What reserve would reduce that risk this week?

> [!question] For Educators And Advisors
> What backup channel, practice space, or partner relationship should students know about before they need it?
