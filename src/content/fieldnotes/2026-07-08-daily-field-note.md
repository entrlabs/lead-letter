> [!quote] Field Note
> Resilience is not a speech after disruption. It is the backup system built before people need it.

> [!summary] The Reader Promise
> Service fails when leaders promise more than the system can actually carry. The better move is to know the boundary early: where the normal channel breaks, where customers lose trust, where students need practice, and where institutions need help before pressure arrives.

## The Field Note

> [!example] Capacity-Aware Service
> Service-minded leadership starts with the people most harmed when the normal system stops working.
>
> A humane backup plan is not a luxury; it is evidence that leaders understood the real dependency.
>
> Students, founders, and managers all need practice translating pressure into concrete support, not just better language.

Promises become fragile when leaders do not know the edge of the system. A benefit channel can break. A service desk can trap customers. A public-finance plan can outrun capacity. A learning program can sound practical while still failing to give students real practice.

The service question is simple: what can this system actually handle, and where does it need backup, partnership, practice, or restraint?

## The Farnam Lens

> [!tip] Thinking Tool: Circle Of Competence
> **Mental model:** [Circle of Competence: How to Find Your Edge](https://fs.blog/circle-of-competence/)  
> **Useful idea:** Today's sources reward leaders who know what their system can actually handle: SNAP replacement, customer service, fiscal promises, hands-on learning, and grant governance all fail when leaders pretend capacity is larger than it is.  
> **Short quote:** "Knowing its boundaries, however, is vital."  
> **Use today:** Ask a class, founder team, or manager group to name one boundary they must respect before scaling a promise.

> [!note] Why It Matters
> Knowing the boundary is not pessimism. It is the beginning of better service. Once leaders can name the edge, they can build partnerships, backups, training, and expectations that people can trust.

## Use It Today

> [!question] For Students
> Where are you being asked to perform beyond your current practice? What support or repetition would make the promise real?

> [!question] For Managers And Founders
> What customer, employee, or community promise sits closest to the edge of your real capacity?

> [!question] For Educators And Advisors
> Which student-success promise needs a backup channel, a local partner, or more hands-on practice before pressure arrives?
