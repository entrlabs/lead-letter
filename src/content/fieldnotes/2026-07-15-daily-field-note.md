> [!quote] Field Note
> Starting is not the same as sustaining. Good leaders build the extra room, clear paths, and protected time that help people keep going after the first move.

> [!summary] The Reader Promise
> Momentum can hide weak follow-through. This note shows how leaders can build enough buffer after activity begins so founders, learners, teams, and customers are not left carrying friction alone.

## The Field Note

> [!example] Fund the Follow-Through
> Before celebrating a launch, ask what people need next: time, guidance, pricing clarity, simple re-entry paths, or room to adjust.

New business applications can rise while new firms still face a hard survival test. Tariff costs can move through a company slowly, then land as pricing decisions customers feel.

Adult learners can want to return and still face a maze of rules, offices, and missing support. Workers can need new skills and still have no real time to learn them.

These are follow-through problems. The first move matters, but the system has to keep carrying people after that move begins.

Margin of safety is the useful lens. It means leaving enough room for reality. Leaders apply it by protecting training time, making re-entry simpler, building advice close to founders, and explaining price pressure before trust breaks.

## Mental Model Lens

> [!tip] Thinking Tool: Margin of Safety
> **Mental model:** [Margin of Safety: Expect the Unexpected](https://fs.blog/margin-of-safety/)  
> **Useful idea:** A plan needs extra capacity because real life rarely follows the clean version.  
> **Short quote:** "What if I'm wrong?"  
> **Use today:** Look at one plan that has already started. Name the buffer it needs now: time, money, staffing, guidance, trust, or a backup path.

> [!note] Why It Matters
> Margin of safety keeps leaders from treating early motion as proof that the work will hold. It asks a plainer question: what extra room will people need when the first plan meets pressure?

## Use It Today

> [!question] For Students
> Where do you need more room after the start: time to practice, a clearer path, feedback, or a backup plan?

> [!question] For Managers And Founders
> Which current initiative has energy but too little buffer for pricing pressure, customer confusion, training time, or early operating mistakes?

> [!question] For Educators And Advisors
> Ask learners to map what happens after someone says yes. Where does the process become too hard to finish?
